Publications

My publications can also be viewed on my Google Scholar page.

Because my research is interdisciplinary, I publish across a variety of venues with different norms and conventions. In the interest of providing clear context for these publications, I'm experimenting with using the following symbols to give more information:

  • Publication venue:
    • = Item was published in a journal.
    • = Item was published in a peer-reviewed conference (in computer science we often publish papers this way).
    • = Item was presented at a workshop. Workshops are organized as sub-units within conferences to explore specific themes. Generally, workshops use some sort of peer-review process but it's often lighter than the main conference tracks; work is usually assumed to somewhat be in-progress. Sometimes workshops assign a DOI and sometimes they don't, which is why I list some workshops as publications and others as pre-publication.
  • Other information:
    • = Nominated for best paper award.
    • = Indicates that I gave a talk on this paper (location will be in tool-tip if it's not the same as the publication venue).
    • = Link to poster or slides.
    • = Link to video of talk.
    • = Link to other relevant resources
    • * = denotes undergraduate mentee co-authors
    • = Link to GitHub repo

Peer-reviewed Extended Abstracts
